Introduction

Welcome to the first page of my critical reflective journal, written by Connor Orton BSc. I am a 27-year-old aspiring games programmer who has released 2 published full android games on the Google Play store that is studying an Indie Games Development master’s degree. My journal will document the progress of my practical work, contextual research and discuss my games development practices as I progress throughout my course. I will document my weekly challenges and the spark forums that are used to communicate with my peers to get feedback and to give feedback to others. My posts will be written critically, and I will explain how I think that I’ve accomplished the task then evaluate what I’ve learned in the process.

This blog is designed for educational purposes, and any outsource material will be referenced correctly to the original source, author or creator at the bottom of each post. Below is a diagram that I created of the blog structure which highlights each module and how each post will be laid out in an agile project methodology format as this is how the course is managed. Below the diagram is a contents page that will take you to the specified course module.

GDO710 - Development Practice

This first module is GDO710 Development Practice which is based around practice and reflection where I will be graded by this critical reflective journal and a case study video at the end of the module. The posts I will write will document my prior and current notions of development practice and will show the research, reflection and deliberate practices I have endeavoured. By the end of this module I will have gained the confidence and clarity to state clearly my aspirations after graduating from the course, what skills I need to improve on in order to achieve these aspirations and what medium I need to create to evidence my skillset.
