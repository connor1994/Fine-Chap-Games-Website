Spark Forum

For this week’s spark forum, I was tasked with sharing an experience where I had to complete a challenging task on time and explain my tactics and if I could adapt my tactics to agile methodologies. 

Spark forum for Week 10: Agile Practice (Falmouth University, 2021).

For my bachelor’s degree I was tasked with creating an asset pack for the Unreal Engine marketplace using C++ or Unreal Blueprint in under 3 months while balancing out other assignment deadlines. Because my course was 3D oriented and there was a very little programming learning influence, so I gave myself a project with high scope to learn at a greater depth.

I decided to create an asset pack that recreates the Pokémon franchises game mechanics using unreal blueprint visual scripting. Examples of a few types of game mechanics I had to recreate were the following:
- Dialogue System, the ability to interact with NPCs around the testing area. 
- Combat System, the ability to fight enemy NPCs in a battle system scenario. 
- Shop System, the ability to purchase and sell items from NPCs. 
- Menu System, the ability to manage the player’s creature team, use items, change options and more. 

I was successful in completing this assignment on time by using an Agile project management methodology. To manage my project, just like my two rapid ideation projects I used a Trello, a website for creating a Kanban board which manages various tasks that I had to carry out. I would refer to my Kanban board daily and update my tasks accordingly depending on if they are finished, to do or removed. Kanban has provided the games industry with predictability, transparency and optimization, making it easier to develop complex games without too much stress or overspending on budget (IBQMI, 2021). Going forward into the course I will continue to use a Kanban methodology for all my future game projects as I work the most efficiently whilst keeping focussed and motivated.

The result of my assignment project where I utilized agile project management to complete it under a specific time frame.

Envisioning Process

The envisioning process is a lightweight, iterative design activity that combines user design with technology exploration to result in a vision for the product and a planned roadmap. It will ensure product delivery to the target market through collaboration with end-users and it is a practical way of delivering customer-driven design within an Agile process (Talbott, 2010). The purpose behind using the envisioning process is to produce 3 elements of data which include keeping the vision towards the product, adequate information to know about the features, product conclusion and the stakeholders and finally to estimate the cost of the product (KnowledgeHut, 2021). 

Envisioning process illustration (KnowledgeHut, 2021).

My first idea was to create a pixel art platformer game where you defeat dragons as you progress through the level, but I decided against it because despite the platformer genre not being that difficult to develop the environment art would have taken too much time to create, I needed a level that was small and was static. Regardless, generally it is advised to not go with your first idea when developing a game for a games jam as excitement usually clouds judgement of the idea that is going to be created and more thought must be put into it to get the right scale of scope and feasibility.

Personal Brand

Personal branding is the practice of marketing individuals and their careers as a brand. It is the process of developing and upholding a reputation as an individual or company and how they go about promoting themselves. A brand will showcase unique a combination of skills, experience and personality that followers will see and how the brand’s story is told (Henderson, 2019).

Companies in the video games industry takes branding and marketing very seriously and must consider a few elements. Firstly, logo design is important as it has to be memorable, striking and representative of what the company stands for. Secondly, a company must have a good slogan and tag line which must be short and memorable. Finally, customer personas must be created using a combination of demographic and psychographic information to allow for a clear picture of the target audience to use in marketing (TheLogoCompany, 2021).

Branding illustration (Midaspr Group, 2021).
