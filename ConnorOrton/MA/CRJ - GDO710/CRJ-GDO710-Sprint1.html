Course Structure & Agile Management

The course is structure is divided into four sprints, each which contain 3 weeks each in an Agile management format. The division of the structure is set at a sustainable pace which will disallow myself from burning out and will avoid technical debt, loss of personal motivation and commitment, if this was a real life situation in my own indie games company it could cost the team and organization money loss as signification investment would have been made for the development project so careful structure of project management is essential (Babb, Hoda and Norbjerg, 2014). 

The course structure for the first module (Falmouth University, 2021).

Academic Practice

I understand that I must conduct good academic practice when working on and submitting my assignments. The Falmouth University academic integrity policy states “All use of another person’s work or ideas must be attributed and the sources identified, including where you have paraphrased or summarised. You must make sure that you use the correct techniques for citation and referencing as outlined in the assessment brief.” (Falmouth University, 2020). In my writing and practical work which consists of image and asset creation etc I will ensure to cite the correct author or creator to ensure good academic practice and to give credit where it is due. 

I will ensure that credit is given in relation to my games development practices where required as there has been many cases over the years of lawsuits caused by plagiarism of game content, one in particular being the 1972 lawsuit of Magnavox Vs Atari where Ralph Baer, inventor of the Magnavox Odyssey games console sought legal action against Atari for claiming that the Atari co-founder Nolan Bushnell had copied Ping-Pong and in conclusion Atari continued to sell the game on arcades and home consoles while paying the licensing and royalty fees to Magnavox (Obias, 2014). This event is important to reflect on as a video games programmer and developer it is paramount that I do not attempt to copy or steal any content that I use in my game projects, and I will ensure that I will purchase the appropriate license for assets or them from the ground up to avoid any lawsuits for future practice.

Academic Integrity (Monash University, 2021).

Reflective Writing

On my various posts for my critical reflective journal that I will write I will reflect on my writing and practical work. The basics of reflective writing are to look back at something, analyse the event or idea and then think carefully about what the event or idea means for future professional practice (Hampton, 2010). Using these basics and taking a video game mechanic that I have written for example I will determine if there are more effective or efficient ways of writing the script differently and will apply these changes so that I can improve my skillset for future development practice.

Reflective Writing: a basic introduction (Hampton, 2010).
